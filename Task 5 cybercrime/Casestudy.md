## Title: UPI Payment Fraud Case Study
UPI payment fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive banking information. This type of fraud usually happens through phone calls, fake links, or QR codes.

A typical scenario begins when the victim receives a call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official or customer support agent. The fraudster claims there is an issue with the victim’s account or offers a reward or refund. They then ask the victim to click on a link or scan a QR code. Once the victim follows the instructions and enters their UPI PIN, the money gets transferred from their account to the fraudster’s account.
College students and new digital users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams. People who frequently use online payments are also at higher risk.

The consequences of UPI fraud can be serious, including financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital platforms. In some cases, victims are unable to recover their money.
This case highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using digital payment systems.
